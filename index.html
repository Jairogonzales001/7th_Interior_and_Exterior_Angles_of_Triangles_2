Introduction to Exterior Angles

Recap from Part 1

Remember: The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always 180°.

The Exterior Angle Theorem

An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two remote interior angles.

Today's Learning Objectives

  • Identify and work with exterior angles of triangles
  • Apply the Exterior Angle Theorem
  • Understand the relationship between exterior and remote interior angles
  • Represent angle measures as algebraic expressions

Key Concepts

Exterior Angle

Formed by one side of the triangle and the extension of another side.

Remote interior angles: The two angles NOT adjacent to the exterior angle.

D = A + B

Example

If the remote interior angles are 50° and 70°:

Exterior angle = 50° + 70° = 120°

Why It Works

The exterior angle and adjacent interior angle are supplementary (add to 180°).

Since interior angles sum to 180°, the exterior angle equals the remote interior angles sum.

Key Takeaways
